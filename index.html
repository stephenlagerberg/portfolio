Professional Summary

Experienced Air Traffic Manager and Adjunct Professor with a strong background in aviation operations, air traffic control, and performance psychology. My career spans military, commercial, and academic sectors, with expertise in situational awareness, decision-making, and leadership development. Currently serving as Air Traffic Manager at Robinson Aviation (RVA), Inc., I lead operations at the McAllen, Texas International Airport, ensuring safe and efficient airspace management.

In addition to aviation, I hold a Ph.D. in Performance Psychology and teach undergraduate and graduate psychology courses at Grand Canyon University, focusing on topics such as cognitive neuroscience, coaching, and leadership. My research centers on air traffic controller well-being, stress management, and human resilience, with publications addressing burnout and psychological health in aviation professionals. With experience in public administration, social justice, and military operations, I bring a multifaceted approach to problem-solving, mentorship, and strategic planning. I am passionate about enhancing aviation safety, fostering teamwork, and applying psychological insights to optimize human performance.
