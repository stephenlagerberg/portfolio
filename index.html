About Dr. Stephen E. Lagerberg

I specialize in the most critical variable of any complex system: human performance under pressure.

I hold a Ph.D. in General Psychology with an emphasis in Performance Psychology, but my expertise wasn't solely built in a classroom. It was forged in zero-margin environments. Serving over a decade as a U.S. Navy Air Traffic Controller and currently directing complex airspace as an Air Traffic Manager, I have spent my career managing intense cognitive load and mitigating operational fatigue where split-second decisions matter most.

I combine this boots-on-the-ground operational experience with rigorous academic science. As an Adjunct Professor teaching Cognitive Neuroscience and Sports Psychology, I translate the latest research into actionable, battle-tested strategies for my consulting clients.

Whether I am helping an aerospace organization integrate AI safely into their workflows, or advising an operations executive on burnout prevention and cognitive fatigue, my goal is the same: to protect your human capital, eliminate mental friction, and sustain elite output when failure is not an option.
